1st Day Hack

Malaysia's first Highschool Machine Learning Hackathon

Taylor's University Lakeside Campus

21-22 March 2020

What is 1stDayHack?

1stDayHack is one of the few hackathons in Malaysia that caters only to secondary high school students. We want to inspire, engage and give hands-on experience to the next generation of Malaysian youths in the cutting edge of modern computing technology - Machine Learning (ML) . 60 students from various schools, public and private, across 2 days to learn and develop softwares that are highly practical and technologically sophisticated, not merely a coding exercise. Included in the program are various talks, dedicated workshops and tutorials to provide sufficient foundation in topics such as fundamentals of programming and product design. In addition, a dedicated mentor will be assigned to each team to guide them throughout the entire experience.

Tentative Tracks

The theme for the hackathon will be Practical Machine Learning. Therefore, 1stDayHack will be a problem-centric hackathon. Each team will first choose a particular topical issue from a pre-defined pool of affairs that their products and solutions will address. Thereafter, the participants will work alongside their teammates and team-mentor to ideate and rapidly prototype a Machine Learning based solution with their learnings from earlier workshops, and by leveraging on a set of pre-prepared ML-toolkit. More importantly, the teams will need to be able to create a compelling pitch deck and deliver a thoughtful pitch to show how their products and solutions will help address the issue that they have set out to tackle. The following are some examples of what the participants will be able to create on the day. More details and specifics of the tracks and tools will be provided in the weeks leading up to 1stDayHack and during the hackathon brief on the day itself.

Online social media sentiment analysis with Natural Language Processing (NLP)

Highly competitive object classifier with Deep Convolutional Neural Networks

Automated web crawler for online products aggregation and analysis

FAQ

Am I elligible for the Hackathon?

1stDayHack is open to all secondary school students ranging from the age of 14 to 18. The miminum age is enforced as we estimate that the materials will be too difficult for students below said age. We recommend that participants register as a group as the hackathon requires partipants to work in a team of 5, though individuals are welcomed too. We will match up the individual participants without a team on the day itself according to the availability.

I am a school teacher. How can I sign my students up?

Please sign up with the form as provided under Where can I sign up? . The hackathon will be conducted based on a team system with 5 students per team. Therefore, you are encouraged to form the teams amongst your students first before signing up. You can register more than 1 team from your school.

Do I need to pay to participate?

Yes, a fee of RM 20 per participant is required on sign-up. We have endeavored to make this as cheap as possible as our intention is to educate. The fee affords us sufficient finances just to cover our operational costs. We do not take any profit from the fees.

Where can I sign up?

You can initiate the sign-up process by completing this form for schools, and this form for individual participants. Thereafter, we will reach out to you to follow up on the payment, administrative requirements and/or group arrangements as part of the standard procedure. Please allow us a few days to reach out to you and feel free to contact us through our email during this period.

How can I make the payment?

Payment can be made through direct bank transfers to our Maybank account. For security purposes, details of said bank account will be emailed directly to you upon completing the sign-up form. We understand that you could be wary of direct account transactions, but this allows us to minimize transactions charges usually incured by third-party channels. If you require any further verification for your ease of mind, feel free to reach out to us through our email and we would be happy to help.

Will accommdation or transportation be provided?

No, accommdation and transportation will have to be accounted for by the teacher/parent/guardian of the participants. There are various lodgings available around the Taylor's Lakeside Campus as it is right next to the Sunway region. Teacher/parent/guardian are expected to drop-off and pick-up their charges at the beginning and end of both day.

Will meals be provided?

No, meals will not be provided but teacher/parent/guardian will not need worry on the availability of food and beverages. There are many restaurants, cafes and convenient stores available on campus that will be able to cater to your catering needs.

What do I need to bring?

Each participants will need to bring along their own laptop and chargers for the hackathon. Other materials such as pen and paper are not compulsory but recommended. Please also ensure that each participant brings along their IC or student ID card for verification purposes.

How can I prepare my child or students for the hackathon?

It is highly recommended that the participant be comfortable with basic computing and computer programming, especially in utilizing the Python programming language. Knowledge of Machine Learning though vastly helpful, will not be required. We will provide a list of curated resources that could help you get them started on our website soon.

As a Teacher/Parent/Guardian, can I stay to observe my charges?

Yes you can. We may limit the amount of observers per party on the day itself according to the availability of venue space.

When will registrations close?

We will close registration two week from the hackathon itself.

Questions not in FAQ

Please reach out to us through our email at 1stdayhack@gmail.com for any further inquiries.

Recommended Resources

Please note the following are optional - parents and teachers that would want to better prepare their child for the hackathon could get them started first with the following resources.

[Core]
Python Programming

This is the most important pre-requisite that we recommend each participant to pay heed to. We will have a simple workshop that will provide a crash course on this topic during the first day of 1stDayHack. However, it will still be much more conducive if each participant brushes up on the topic before attending the hackathon.

We recommend any one of the following resources for self-studying Python Programming.

  • LearnPython(Free, Medium Difficulty)
  • CodeAcademy(Paid, Easy Difficulty)
  • DataCamp(Paid, Easy Difficulty)

[Core]
Programming with Jupyter-Notebooks

For 1stDayHack, we will be using Jupyter-Notebook based kernels (programming environment) for the lessons and development. Specifically, Kaggle's Kernel will be our main programming environment as Kaggle provides up to 30 hours of FREE GPU hours on powerful NVIDIA GPUs - useful for ML development.

We recommend the following resources to get familiar with Notebook-based enviroments.

  • Jupyter's homepage. Useful references.
  • DataQuest's quick intro to Jupyter-Notebooks.
  • Sample Kaggle Kernels. Try executing pre-written codes and models in Kaggle's Kernel directly.

[Secondary]
Fundamentals of Machine Learning

This is a highly optional recommendation as most of the Machine Learning materials online may be too difficult for secondary students to read up alone. However, if you are technically competent, feel free to give them a go!

We recommend any one of the following resources for self-studying introductory Machine Learning.

  • Kaggle's intro to ML (Free, Easy Difficulty)
  • Google's own crash course. Much more complete than Kaggle's (Free, Hard Difficulty)
  • PyTorch's getting started in Deep Learning with PyTorch (Free, Very Hard Difficulty)
