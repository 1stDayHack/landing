WHY SECONDARY SCHOOLERS?

Bandwidth. We will need more than 750,000 additional digital talents by 2020 alone. This fact would be immediately obvious to any tech-inclined companies. Demand exceed supply despite a decade of record high enrolment rate to CS programs at universities. Thus, the problem is one of bandwidth.

In Malaysia, most of the current developers and coders are capable of carrying out professional projects are churned out from universities alone and that is problematic. Coding is actually not hard to learn, and most certainly do not require a 4-year degree to be proficient at. In fact, it has been made much easier nowadays thanks to the countless resources available online - all one needs is an interest to get started, and perseverance to get through the initial learning hump.

That is why the secondary school would be a great place to get them started. We believe that there are many talented to-be developers in Malaysia that we can help kickstart and nurture through this initiative.

WHAT’S DIFFERENT ABOUT 1STDAYHACK?

Try searching for “Highschool Hackathons”. What you will find would most likely be 2 things. An abundance of such hackathons overseas and next to none in Malaysia. In fact, we have found no promising hackathons dedicated to middle and highschool-ers in Malaysia at all. That is exactly what 1stDayHack is for.

The hackathon will be organized so that every student will, if nothing at all, be able to takeaway with them 2 things - learning how to code practically, and a simple functional tech product. The usual loose approach of hackathons may be too undirected for young beginners such that most will finish the day with nothing in hand. Instead, 1stDayHack will have well-defined tracks with accompanying recommended projects. This way, tools and effective mentorship could be prepared in advance to facilitate their learning. To emphasize, the main purpose is to get them building actual products practically and as quick as possible.

Tentative Tracks

The theme for the hackathon will be Practical Machine Learning.

Online social media sentiment analysis with Natural Language Processing (NLP)

Highly competitive object classifier with Deep Convolutional Neural Networks

Automated web crawler for online products aggregation and analysis
